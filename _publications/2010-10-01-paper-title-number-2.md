But how can we solve this dilema?

I'm trying to understant the psychological impact of trading. Sometimes I notice I trade 
very infrequently-Just once or twice a week. That can be frustrating because I feel like i'm 
missing out on opportunities, leaving money on the table. But at the same time, being overly
cautious might be beneficial since I'm in a positive state of return. This fear of missing 
trades also mixes with a deep-seated fear of losing what I've already gained. Ghosts of 
past losses make me hesitant. So, how can I resolve this internal conflict?

This dilemma is often tied to what's called performance anxiety or the sunkcost fallacy. One
way to adress it is by anchoring yourself to a set trading plan: define entry and exit 
rules that aren't driven by the number of trades but by signal quality. You can also 
normalize the fact that fewer trades if well-sellected, often yield better risk-adjusted
returns. And it can help to remind youself that preservation of capital if often more valuable
than chasing every single opportunity.
